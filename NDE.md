## Non-Destructive Evaluation (NDE) Methods  
**Institution:** Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University  
**Dates:** October 2025  
**Course:** AE 417 - Aerospace Structures & Instrumentation Lab
**Equipment & Tools:** Borescope, Infrared Camera, X-Ray Imager, Fluorescent Liquid Penetrant, Ultrasonic Inspection Device

---

## Experiment Overview  

This experiment introduced a variety of non-destructive evaluation (NDE) techniques used to detect surface & internal defects in engineering materials without causing damage. The lab procedures emphasized qualitative understanding of how different inspection methods interact with materials to reveal flaws.

The lab covered five primary NDE methods:

- Visual Inspection  
- Thermography (Infrared Imaging)  
- Radiography (X-ray Imaging)  
- Liquid Penetrant Inspection  
- Ultrasonic Testing  

Each technique demonstrated a unique physical principle for identifying defects such as cracks, voids, and material inconsistencies in aerospace structures.

---

## Procedure & Results  

Each NDE method was performed in a station-based format, allowing for expedited experimentation & direct comparison of each NDE technique.

### Visual Inspection  
A borescope & optical tools were used to inspect both accessible & shielded regions of damaged aerospace components as seen below.



- Identified surface-level defects such as cracks, corrosion, and deformation  
- Demonstrated limitations of human vision and the need for optical aids  
- Highlighted the importance of inspection angles and lighting conditions  

---

### Thermography  
An infrared camera was used to observe temperature variations across different materials and objects.

- Detected thermal gradients indicating material differences or heat flow  
- Visualized hidden features through temperature contrast  
- Showed sensitivity to environmental conditions and surface properties  

---

### Radiography (X-ray Imaging)  
Pre-captured X-ray images were analyzed to study internal features of test specimens.

- Revealed internal structures such as voids, inclusions, and embedded objects  
- Demonstrated how material density affects X-ray attenuation  
- Provided high-resolution insight into internal defects not visible externally  

---

### Liquid Penetrant Inspection  
A dye penetrant system was applied to metallic components to highlight surface cracks.

- Capillary action drew penetrant into small surface discontinuities  
- Developer solution enhanced visibility of defects under proper lighting  
- Effectively revealed fine cracks not visible to the naked eye  

---

### Ultrasonic Testing  
An ultrasonic probe was used to send high-frequency sound waves through test blocks.

- Detected internal features via reflected wave signals (echo patterns)  
- Identified discontinuities such as holes or material boundaries  
- Demonstrated sensitivity to probe placement and coupling quality
